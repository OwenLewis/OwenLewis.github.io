Math 312 Partial Differential Equations for Engineering

Schedule: Section 01: MWF 2:00 - 2:50

This semester I am teaching Math 312: Partial Differential Equations for Engineering. Material covered will include: Solution methods for partial differential equations; science and engineering applications; heat and wave equations, Laplace's equation; separation of variables; Fourier series and transforms; special functions. I will be using Canvas to communicate with my students and distribute course materials. Please contact me if you are enrolled and do not have access to the Canvas site.

About Owen

My name is Owen Lewis. I am currently an Assistant Professor in the Mathematics & Statistics department at the University of New Mexico. Previously I was a Dean's Post-doctoral scholar at Florida State University where I worked with Dr. Nick Cogan. Prior to that, I was a Research Assistant Professor in the Mathematical Biology Research Group at the University of Utah where I worked with Dr. Aaron Fogelson and Dr. James Keener. I received my Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from the University of California Davis where I worked under Dr. Robert Guy.

When not working, I like to spend my time playing in the mountains (or anywhere outdoors). In my previous homes, I spent a large amount of time in the Wasatch and Sierra Nevada mountains. I am an avid alpine and backcountry skier, and missed the snow dearly when living in Florida. I am very excited about my new home in New Mexico. When there is no snow to be found I enjoy backpacking and hiking whenever possible. I am also an avid sports fan. Before you ask: Forty Niners, Trail Blazers, Arsenal.
